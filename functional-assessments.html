Functional Assessments

Measure a person's level of function and ability to perform functional or work-related tasks safely and reliably, performed by registered and trained functional assessors.

What Are Functional Assessments?

Functional assessments are performed by registered and trained functional assessors to measure a person's level of function and ability to perform functional or work-related tasks safely and reliably. These assessments provide objective, evidence-based data that is critical for informed decision-making.

The assessor collects data during evaluation and from referral materials to analyse the individual's functional capacity. This includes a thorough review of clinical information, direct observation, and standardised testing to build a complete picture of the person's abilities and limitations.

Functional assessments serve both pre-employment screening and post-injury evaluation roles, making them a versatile and essential tool for employers, insurers, and legal professionals alike.

Key Benefits

  • Mitigate the risk of causing injury to employees through evidence-based screening
  • Ensure injured workers maintain recovery progress with objective measurements
  • Pre-employment screening to assess candidate suitability for specific roles
  • Post-injury evaluation for return-to-work planning and compensation claims

Assessment Components

What Is Analysed

Each functional assessment includes multiple rigorous components to deliver a comprehensive evaluation of the individual's capabilities.

Clinical Pathology Examination

Thorough clinical review to understand the underlying pathology and its impact on functional capacity.

Client Behaviour Analysis

Observation and analysis of client behaviours that impact physical performance during the assessment process.

Musculoskeletal Testing

Objective musculoskeletal, neuromuscular, and biomechanical testing using standardised protocols and validated tools.

Functional Testing

Direct assessment of the individual's ability to perform specific functional and work-related tasks safely and reliably.

Effort Assessment

Evaluation of consistency and effort levels throughout the assessment to ensure validity and reliability of results.

Pre-Employment Screening

Functional assessments play a critical role in pre-employment screening by objectively measuring a candidate's ability to safely perform the physical demands of a role. This helps employers mitigate the risk of causing injury to employees by ensuring candidates are physically suited to the job requirements.

By identifying potential risks before placement, employers can make informed hiring decisions and implement appropriate accommodations where needed.

Post-Injury Evaluation

Following a workplace injury, functional assessments provide objective evidence of a worker's current capacity and limitations. This data is essential for developing safe return-to-work plans that match the individual's capabilities.

By ensuring injured workers maintain recovery progress, these assessments protect both the employee and employer while supporting compensation claims with credible, objective data.
