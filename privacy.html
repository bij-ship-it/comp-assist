Privacy Policy

How Compensation Assistance Services collects, uses, and protects your personal information.

Protecting Your Privacy

Compensation Assistance Services (CAS) and Comp Assist Rehab aligns its privacy framework with the 13 Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) contained in the Privacy Act 1988. These principles, which took effect on 12 March 2014, govern the collection, use, disclosure, storage, security, and disposal of personal information.

For more information about the Australian Privacy Principles, visit the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner at oaic.gov.au.

Collection of Personal Information

CAS collects information reasonably necessary for undertaking functions as a provider with Regulatory Bodies such as WorkCover NSW and Comcare. We gather data from multiple sources — the injured person and various stakeholders — to provide evidence-based workplace rehabilitation services.

Types of Personal Information Collected

  • Name, address, and date of birth
  • Next-of-kin details
  • Claim-related information and injury factors
  • Medical treatment history
  • Previous and current medical conditions
  • Work experience and employment details
  • Information affecting work capacity

Consent: Written consent is required from all clients. Refusal to provide consent may prevent service delivery, and referring parties may be notified of this decision.

Sensitive Information

Sensitive information includes details regarding racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, membership of a political association, religious or philosophical beliefs, membership of a trade union, and health information.

CAS collects sensitive information only when reasonably necessary for the services requested or being delivered, with client consent or as required by law. Health information collection follows the Workplace Injury Management and Worker's Compensation Act 1998.

Information Disclosure

Personal information may be disclosed to:

Treating professionals, employers, and WorkCover agents
WorkCover NSW, Comcare, insurers, and solicitors
Other rehabilitation consultants or providers
As required by law, court subpoena, or legal requirements

Disclosure may also occur for the prevention of serious or imminent health threats, or for the investigation of suspected unlawful activity.

Security of Personal Information

Your data receives protection from misuse and loss and from unauthorized access, modification, or disclosure. CAS maintains a secure, password-protected electronic database with electronic logging to prevent unauthorized tampering.

Data Retention

Most personal information remains in client files for a minimum of 7 years. Information no longer needed undergoes destruction or permanent de-identification.

Data Quality

Reasonable steps are taken to ensure personal information is accurate, complete, and current. Files transition to secure archival storage when inactive.

Access to Personal Information

Under APP 12 and 13, individuals may access their held personal information and request corrections, subject to certain exceptions.

How to Request Access

  • 1. Submit a written request to the General Manager
  • 2. Response is typically provided within 30 calendar days
  • 3. Fees may apply for staff time locating, retrieving, and reproducing information (communicated in advance)

CAS corrects information when satisfied that it is inaccurate, out of date, incomplete, irrelevant, or misleading. Clients should notify us of changes regarding work capacity, attendance ability, and contact details. No charges apply for corrections or quality reviews.
